STANLEY GAS/CONDENSATE PROJECT

The Stanley gas/condensate field is located in the Western Province, 40 kilometers NW from Kiunga. The well was first discovered in 1999 by Santos. The contingent resources are 355 bcf gas and 11 mmbbls of condensate. 

The Stanley Project development concept contemplates initial condensate production of 4,000 barrels per day utilising a two train refrigeration plant located in the field. The condensate will be transported via a 6 inch, 40 kilometre pipeline to a 6,000 barrel storage tank at Kiunga base then loaded onto a specially designed tanker with a 33,000 barrel capacity. The gas will be re-injected and sold later when the market becomes available.

The APDL and APL were lodged with the Department on 28 August 2012. DPE did all the necessary reviews, and after the Gas Agreement was signed, the Department including relevant State proceeded and conducted the development forum in Kiunga.

The forum concluded on 30thMay 2014 with the signing of the Development Agreement and the Petroleum Minister Hon. Nixon Duban ,issued the PDL and PL to the project proponents on the same day.  
Construction and development of the project has not started yet due to the issue of operatorship between Horizon and Talisman. The matter was resolve in 26th January 2015, when Talisman was formally recognized as the operator of the project. 

It is now agreed that the project development would be differed for at least a year as Talisman will have to undertake a review of the development due to the fall in oil prices.
Some urgent tasks on the project are yet to be resolved to allow the project to commence operations without hindrance. The Department is continuously working with other State agencies to resolve them.

 

 

KETU/ELEVALA GAS CONDENSATE PROJECT

The Ketu and Elevala fields consist of over 1 trillion cubic feet (tcf) of gas and approximately 55 million barrels (mmbbls) of condensate. 

The Project Development Concept contemplates a gas condensate development focused on near term condensate recovery with gas sales to follow. The condensate will be processed in the fields and transported by pipeline to Kiunga for export. The gas will be re-injected and sold later as LNG.

The Department received the application for a petroleum development license (APDL) from Horizon Oil on 17th March 2014. However because of indifferences between JV partners in the lodgement of the APDL, DPE advised Horizon to meet with all its JV partners to reach a consensus before DPE can proceed with the review of the application.

A project Team was formed in August 2014 to review the APDL and APL applications. The review plus all other associated work leading up to grant of the PDL and commencement of the project have also not started due to no funding support from the Government. The Department has phased the work program due to this situation but even the reduced work program has not been funded.

Social mapping and landowner identification studies was conducted by the Licensees under section 47 Oil & Gas Act 1998. Landowner beneficiary identification is currently in progress. The Technical review (sub-surface and facilities) has been completed by NCPC . The APDL review lacks progress in other key project activities such as legal, economics and environmental reviews. Subsequently, National Content Plan is still outstanding.

 

 

GOBE OIL PROJECT

The Gobe Oil Project is located in the Southern Highlands Province and comprises two producing oil fields, the South East (SE) Gobe oil field and the Gobe Main oil field.

SE Gobe was discovered in early 1991 and Gobe Main was discovered in 1993 and is approximately 5 kilometers from north west of the SE Gobe.

The Gobe Main field is located within Petroleum Development Licence 4 (PDL 4), while SE Gobe straddles the boundary of PDL-4 and adjoining PDL-3. The project was initially operated by Chevron but later sold to Oil Search who owns and operate the project.
Oil is exported via an 8 kilometre pipeline which joins the Gobe Processing Facility to the Kutubu Export Pipeline and marine loading terminal in the Gulf of Papua. The Project was completed in early 1998 and commercial production from the Gobe Main field began in March 1998 and SE Gobe began in April 1998.

Production peaked in December 1998 at 18,500 barrels per day from SE Gobe and at 20,000 barrels per day from Gobe Main in September 1999. Production has declined since and is now producing at an average of 5,000 barrels of Oil per day.

 

MORAN OIL PROJECT

The Moran Oil Project covers three licence areas, namely PDL 2, PDL 5 and PDL 6 and is located in the Hela Province. Oil was discovered in mid 1996 by Chevron Niugini and production commenced in January 1998.

The project was initially operated by Chevron but later Oil Search bought out Chevron assets.

Moran production is processed at the Agogo Production Facility (APF) and liquids are then piped to the Central Production Facility for further processing, storage and export through the export pipeline. 

In late 2003, NW Moran, an extension of the Moran field towards the north west, into PPL 219, was discovered. In September 2005, NW Moran 1 well commenced, following the construction of a 23 kilometre pipeline linking NW Moran into the APF. 

This has resulted in an increase in production rates in recent years. A Production Development Licence over the NW Moran field, PDL 6, was awarded in 2008. 

Moran processes crude and is similar to Gobe and Kutubu crude and is sold as “Kutubu Blend”.

 

SE MANANDA PROJECT

The SE Mananda development came on-stream in early 2006 after a detailed technical and engineering studies by Oil Search in 2003 that confirmed the field to be economical.

Initially operated by Chevron, it is now owned and operated by Oil Search

There were significant challenges associated with the development, including very difficult terrain and the need to span the 470 metre wide, 400 metre deep Hegigio Gorge. 

The latter resulted in the design and construction of the world's longest pipeline suspension bridge. In addition, the SE Mananda landowners are made up of three separate ethnic groups, which under Oil Search, for the first time came together in a cooperative manner. The infrastructure will facilitate further development opportunities along the Mananda ridge.

The Initial production was at an average of 3 000 barrels per day . Production has declined since and is now producing at an average of 500 barrels  per day
