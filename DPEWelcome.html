Message from the Minister

Welcome to the launch of the brand new DPE Website.

I am pleased to present the 2015 updates on all Oil and Gas Projects and other Institutional Projects currently being overseen by my Ministry and being implemented by the Department of Petroleum & Energy.

It is necessary that I inform the Government, the people of PNG and all concerned stakeholders the status and progress of key oil and gas projects that currently underpin the economy and the progress of development in PNG.

The Government, through my Ministry and the Department has delivered the first PNG LNG shipment of the country’s LNG exports to the Asian markets, in May 2014. In the same year (2014) in April, we delivered a new Gas (Condensate) Project in Western Province – the Stanley (Gas) Condensate Project. We are also hopeful that with the inclusion of Total SA as a Joint Venture partner in the PRL 15 Elk/Antelope Project, we will announce a third and LNG Project in 2015. Hence, we will work towards achieving a PDL, through the normal regulatory processes. The PRL 3 Pynang Project is defined as an acceleration project or development by the Operator – ExxonMobil. The Government is mindful of the Pynang Project MOU which was signed in January 2015. The Government is focused in Domestic Gas (DomGas) utilisation and the anticipation of producing a 70 Mega Watt power generation from domestic gas by June 2015 is not farfetched. The Pynang MOU sets the general intentions that will follow the normal legal and regulatory requirements, under Oil & Gas Act 1998.

The Ministry and the Department is also currently working with Twinza Oil to develop the Pasca oil and gas condensate Project by 2018. Pasco project will be the first offshore Oil and gas project in the country. With increased interest by world major oil and gas companies in taking acreage in PNG, this has set an increased demand on the Department to review its Licensing processes and also on inspections and audits of current projects. The Department in its current form cannot adequately address the petroleum industry demands and key stakeholders’ issues and programs, such as the landowners and Provincial Governments.

The decision of the NEC in its Decision No: 145/2013 to trans-migrate the Department into Petroleum and Energy Authority, necessitates DPE’s operational mechanism through the formation of a structure in accordance with the requirements of the Public Service (Management) Act and the General Orders before transiting into the Authority. With the intention to address the government’s strategic objectives, the revised Structure has to create a formidable framework to respond to the increasing demands and challenges of key stakeholders and the oil and gas developers.

In accordance with the above NEC Decision DPE has begun the transmigrating process by initiating the establishing the Petroleum Advisory Council and the Energy Advisory Council. This will lead to the abolishing of the Department of Petroleum and Energy. It is envisaged that the proposed structure is setting a more vivid focus on its implementation strategies considering the enormity of the workload in the petroleum industry that increased over the years. Such intervention will simultaneously create potential opportunities for enhancement of the institutional capacities of the workforce.

My Ministry and Department will continue to provide revised updates to keep the Government, the people of this country and all concerned stakeholders fully informed of projects and issues of national interest from time to time.

 

Hon. Nixon Duban, Minister for Petroleum & Energy
